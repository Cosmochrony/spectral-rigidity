\section{Completeness via Klein}
  \label{sec:klein}

  The three cases in Theorem~\ref{thm:gram-rigidity} together cover all finite subgroups
  of $\SU(2)$ admissible under spectral neutrality.
  The argument is a triple exclusion against the full Klein list:
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item $\Z_n$ is excluded by Step~2: cyclic groups have no traceless elements for $n \ge 3$,
    and $\Z_1, \Z_2$ cannot be generated by any element.
    \item $2T$ is excluded by Proposition~\ref{prop:2T-excluded}: the full set of
    traceless elements of $2T$ generates only $Q_8$, so $2T$ admits no neutral
    generating set.
    \item $\mathrm{Dic}_n$ for $n \ge 3$ falls into case~(i) and is correctly
    classified by anisotropy.
    \item $Q_8 = \mathrm{Dic}_2$ falls into case~(ii).
    \item $2O$ and $2I$ fall into case~(iii).
  \end{enumerate}
  No group remains unaccounted for.
  The admissible finite subgroups of $\SU(2)$ under spectral neutrality are exactly
  \begin{equation}
    Q_8,\quad \mathrm{Dic}_n\;(n \ge 3),\quad 2O,\quad 2I.
    \label{eq:admissible-list}
  \end{equation}
