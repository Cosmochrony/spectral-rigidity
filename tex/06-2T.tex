\section{Structural exclusion of $2T$}
  \label{sec:2T}

  \begin{proposition}[Exclusion of $2T$]
    \label{prop:2T-excluded}
    The binary tetrahedral group $2T$ admits no symmetric neutral generating set.
    Consequently, $A_4$ cannot appear as the projective image $\rhobar(G)$
    of any neutral generating set in an irreducible $2$-dimensional representation.
  \end{proposition}

  \begin{proof}
    The group $2T$ has order~24 and decomposes as
    \begin{align}
      \text{Coset~1 (}Q_8\text{):}\quad
      &\{\pm 1,\; \pm i,\; \pm j,\; \pm k\},
      \label{eq:2T-Q8} \\
      \text{Coset~2:}\quad
      &\left\{\tfrac{\pm 1 \pm i \pm j \pm k}{2}\right\}
      \quad \text{(all $16$ sign combinations)}.
      \label{eq:2T-coset2}
    \end{align}
    In the spin-$\tfrac{1}{2}$ representation, the character of a quaternion
    $q = a + bi + cj + dk$ is $\chi(q) = \Tr(\rho(q)) = 2a$.
    Hence $\chi(q) = 0$ if and only if $a = 0$.
    Among elements of Coset~1, $a = 0$ only for $\{\pm i, \pm j, \pm k\}$
    (since $\pm 1$ have $a = \pm 1$).
    Every element of Coset~2 has $|a| = \tfrac{1}{2} \neq 0$.

    Therefore the set of all traceless elements of $2T$ is exactly
    $T_0 := \{\pm i, \pm j, \pm k\} = Q_8 \setminus \{\pm 1\}$.
    This set generates $Q_8$ (order~8), not $2T$ (order~24).
    Any symmetric neutral generating set for $2T$ must consist of traceless
    elements, hence $\langle S \rangle \subset \langle T_0 \rangle = Q_8 \subsetneq 2T$,
    so $S$ cannot generate $2T$.
  \end{proof}

  \begin{remark}
    The exclusion of $2T$ is a structural consequence of the quaternionic
    coset decomposition: the non-identity elements of the cyclic part $\langle a \rangle$
    in $\mathrm{Dic}_n$ (for $n \ge 2$) are traceless only at specific orders, but
    in $2T$ the entire second coset has $|a| = 1/2$, placing it irreversibly outside
    the neutral sector.
  \end{remark}
